How Customers Are Supported

Decisions are made around operations and risk. The workflow is simple: restore function first, prevent repeat failures, and plan capital only when it’s justified.

1) Stabilize the Pain Points

  • Rapid assessment, then route the issue to the right technicians with the right parts.
  • Scope, pricing, and schedule windows defined up front; safe temporary measures if needed.
  • Tidy close-out with notes/photos and immediate next steps.

2) Prevent Repeat Failures (PM's That Matters)

  • Right-sized, seasonal PM aligned to the building’s schedule to cut downtime.
  • Documented readings and simple checklists for clear decisions.
  • Small corrective items found during PM are priced and scheduled before they become outages.

3) Plan Capital (Only When It Makes Sense)

  • Side-by-side options with plain pros/cons: downtime, lead times, energy, controls, compliance.
  • End-to-end coordination when chosen: submittals, crane day, LOTO/hot work, controls, commissioning.
  • Reuse viable components where sensible to control cost without sacrificing reliability.

Always-On Fundamentals

  • Facility-first scheduling (bells, services, resident needs).
  • Safety and compliance are non-negotiable.
  • Single point of contact, clear updates, tidy documentation.
