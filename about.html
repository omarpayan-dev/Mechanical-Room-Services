About Omar Payan

Light Commercial HVAC & Plumbing — Central Texas

Who I Am

Service Sales with a Facilities Mindset

Central Texas facilities get dependable outcomes without the runaround. Experience spans 12+ years across San Antonio, New Braunfels, San Marcos, and Austin—supporting schools, military sites, healthcare, worship, multifamily, and commercial offices. Prior U.S. Air Force Staff Sergeant service shaped the approach: straight talk, clear plans, and follow-through.

Support focuses on arranging the right services at the right time: preventative maintenance first, fast repair response when needed, and well-managed replacements that respect schedules, budgets, and site rules.

How Customers Are Supported

  • Preventative Maintenance First
    • Programs coordinated around instruction, services, and resident needs to reduce downtime.
    • Documented readings and simple checklists for confident decisions.
  • Repairs When Needed
    • Issues triaged quickly and routed to the right technicians with the right parts.
    • Clear communication: scope, pricing, schedule, and close-out.
  • Replacements Managed End-to-End
    • From equipment selection to crane day, controls, and commissioning—details handled start to finish.
    • Where practical, existing components reused to control cost without sacrificing reliability.

Working Style & Process

  • Direct & Organized — clear scopes, transparent pricing, realistic timelines.
  • Facility-First Scheduling — planned around bell schedules, services, and resident comfort.
  • Safety & Compliance — permits, LOTO, hot work, medgas protocols, and site rules.
  • Lifecycle Thinking — recommendations weigh total cost of ownership.
  • 1) Listen & Walk — goals, constraints, comfort/hot-water priorities.
  • 2) Document — models/serials, controls, photos, safety/permit needs.
  • 3) Plan — PM cadence, repair path, or replacement options (pros/cons, lead times).
  • 4) Execute — coordinate techs, lifts, materials, access rules.
  • 5) Verify — start-up, readings/trending, concise close-out.
