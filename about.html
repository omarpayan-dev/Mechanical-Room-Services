About Omar Payan

Light Commercial HVAC & Plumbing — Central Texas

Who I Am

Service Sales with a Facilities Mindset

Based in Central Texas, the focus is on helping schools, military facilities, healthcare, worship, multifamily, and commercial offices get dependable HVAC and plumbing outcomes without the runaround. Experience spans 12+ years in service companies across San Antonio, Austin, and the I-35 corridor. Prior service as a U.S. Air Force Staff Sergeant shaped the approach: straight talk, clear plans, and follow-through.

Customers count on streamlined coordination—preventative maintenance first, fast repair response when needed, and well-managed replacements that respect schedules, budgets, and site rules.

How Customers Are Supported

  • Preventative Maintenance First
    • Programs coordinated around bell schedules, services, and patient/resident needs.
    • Documented readings and clear checklists for confident decisions.
  • Repairs When You Need Them
    • Issues triaged quickly and routed to the right technicians with the right parts.
    • Communication stays tight: scope, pricing, schedule, and close-out.
  • Replacements Managed End-to-End
    • From equipment selection to crane day, controls, and commissioning—details handled start to finish.
    • Where practical, existing components reused to control cost without sacrificing reliability.

What I Work On

  • Rooftop Units (RTUs) — PM programs, repairs, replacements with controls integration.
  • Commercial Water Heaters — maintenance, repair coordination, upgrades (pumps, neutralizers, venting).
  • Plumbing & MedGas — preventative checks, emergency response, NFPA-aligned tie-ins with certified partners.
  • Boilers — scheduled maintenance, repair coordination, modernized replacements for efficiency and redundancy.
  • Chillers — seasonal PMs, diagnostics/repairs, replacement management for Texas heat.

Working Style & Values

  • Direct & Organized — scopes are clear, pricing is transparent, and timelines are realistic.
  • Facility-First Scheduling — work is planned around instruction, services, and resident comfort.
  • Safety & Compliance — attention to permits, lockout/tagout, hot work, medgas protocols, and site rules.
  • Lifecycle Thinking — recommendations weigh total cost of ownership, not just today’s fix.

Process — From First Call to Close-Out

  • 1) Listen & Walk the Site — understand goals, constraints, and comfort/hot water pain points.
  • 2) Document — collect model/serials, filters/belts, nameplate data, control integrations, and photos.
  • 3) Plan — outline PM cadence, repair path, or replacement options with pros/cons and lead times.
  • 4) Execute — coordinate techs, lifts, materials, access, and safety requirements.
  • 5) Verify — start-up, trend/measure, and provide a concise close-out with next steps.

Coverage & Availability

Primary coverage includes San Antonio, New Braunfels, San Marcos, and Austin. Project coordination and PM programs are scheduled to minimize disruption. For urgent issues, response is prioritized based on system criticality and occupant impact.

  • Service Windows — standard business hours with after-hours coordination for mission-critical work.
  • Site Access — insured crews, background checks as required, and clear JHAs for specialty tasks.

What to Expect in a Proposal

  • Clear Scope & Pricing — no guesswork; line items reflect the plan discussed on site.
  • Options — preventative maintenance, repair, and replacement paths shown side-by-side when helpful.
  • Schedule — realistic dates, crane day planning (if applicable), and lead-time notes.
  • Safety & Compliance — notes on permits, medgas inspections, or controls integration as needed.
  • Close-Out — readings, photos, and recommendations for what’s next.
